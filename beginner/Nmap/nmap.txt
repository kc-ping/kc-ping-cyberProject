###   Ethical Guidelines and Safe Practice

Always remember that port scanning without permission is illegal. 
Only scan machines you own or are explicitly allowed to test. In a home lab, this means using isolated virtual networks (Host-Only or Internal) so that your scans do not reach any real external systems.
Treat your lab VMs as “your network” and never scan the Internet or other unauthorized targets. Also, avoid aggressive timing flags (-T5) that could overwhelm lab hosts.

## Timing and Performance: 
Flags like -T4 speed up scans, while -v or -vv increase verbosity. For very large ranges, 
-Pn skips host discovery, and --open shows only open ports.

## Scripting & Automation: 
You can write a shell script to run favorite scans or use cron jobs to schedule them. 
For example, add a cron entry (crontab -e) 
    like 0 3 * * 1 nmap -sn 192.168.56.0/24 -oN nmapSummaryCommand.txt to run a weekly host-sweep.
